Yap: Island of Stone Money

What is Money?

There's no Gold or Silver in Yap.

But hundreds of years ago, explorers from Yap found limestone deposits on islands miles and miles away...


And they carved this limetsone into huge stone discs,
And they brought back across the sea on their small bamboo boats
And like many societies, the people of Yap took the stone money they had and decided that was Money...

A piece of Stone Money was really valuable.

Stone Money in different sizes and shaped like a donut

Bank of Stone Money

A few key things about Stone Money:

It is really heavy.

It is used for social transactions - marriage, inheritance, political deals, sign of an alliance, ransom of the battle dead.

It is not used for everyday purchases.

A big piece could weigh more than a car.

The Stone Money does not move.

It's just that everybody in the village knows the stone money has an owner.
